I do not feel human. Not in some fantasy sense, my body is human; rather that I have a constant pain of being fundamentally different.

In a sense, I do not feel completely rendered into the material world, almost as if part of me remains somewhere else, beyond what I can fathom.

It’s like all interaction I have in the material world is done through Sruli Rechts’ Lasting Impression, gloves that prevent me from ever truly feeling this existence. A protective layer preventing me from ever immersing into this temporary phase. The gloves I cannot take off, for it only brings more pain. I understand how I should feel—I fraud my feelings—for when I say that I love, I merely understand that if I were normal, I’d feel love in that moment. This applies to practically all emotions. I understand them, but they do not become one with me. I lack a vital building block that creates me.

I feel a sort of God complex at times, but I know that I am not inherently better. Not as a King is to a peasant, but as a satellite among stars; indifferent at a glance into the sky, but fundamentally different. I speak in the only way people can understand me, language, but it feels like a loss of meaning the second it’s translated to words. Like trying to explain colour to somebody who’s never seen the light, they lack the necessities to even grasp the basics of what you attempt to convey.

Talking to others often feels like traversing through all nature the mother has to offer. Come across all sorts of animals, each acting in their own way. I believe it represents humanity; some come across affable, some may bite, some ambitious to the point of full-circle, scraping at their own flesh without knowing it.

The most basic human concepts, what we teach our infants, is never more than the peak of an animals comprehension. Often, completely beyond it. I feel like a separate entity that cannot properly articulate a message to people, just as a person cannot communicate ideas to a snake.

It hurts. To be so fluent in a language not even the best linguist could decipher. To see patterns and correlation where others see coincidence at best if anything at all. To watch people act frantically trying to escape a labyrinth you mapped in your mind years before they’d even think about such a problem. Often, the labyrinth is too complex for them to understand. More often than not, they become content with the labyrinth; they do not seek the way out as I did.

Part of me envies them—I believe suffering continues after death—I’d at least enjoy this stage if I were as blind as they are. For the rare occurrence where I come across a mind that does not need coaxing, conversation that feels so high in meaning, it usually drains me. I get lost in my thoughts, yet when I finally get the chance to talk about them, I lose all capability, partially due to my inability to translate such thoughts into language.

What I experience phases through me without lingering, which applies to all that I can remember in my life; the memory remained, the meaning was never retained. I feel as if I watch myself externally, with only so much influence on how I act.

This will never change.

Just remember, this is forever.
