He smiled back, unaware—the same empty expression she always wore, like makeup no one could wash off.

Obliviously accepting the quiet glow she'd place in his hands, for her motive wouldn't change the feeling he accepts. An immersive experience, but scripted nonetheless. Soft voices reassure you, do they echo in your mind when the act is over? Everybody he knew moved in steady steps, unphased by change, as if they'd already read the day.

Does a slave with no shackles fight for escape? Everything arrives with no imperfection, memories play tricks and tell you you're the misplaced mind. Each day repeats with little variety, smudged together, faded like chalk on the pavement after the storm passes. Whenever he questioned what it was all for, she stared back blankly, as if language came encrypted.

Life had always been that way, he'd have been insane to question the girl who gave him the only warmth he'd ever known. She knew that. Life to one, an experiment to another. Do you worship her, or the warmth she brings?

Plastic flowers mimicking beauty you could never have for yourself.

The threads below the surface were never spoken—implied with a puppet, constant, only changed by the control of the puppeteer. She appears when the story needs character, with the same shallow voice when the loneliness screams a little too loud, the same shallow voice drowning all else present. The silence felt intentional, like a pause written between lines.

Could he really question all he's ever known? He never understood, but felt he was in no place to understand. Nothing ever forgets itself unless the self is lost.

Sometimes, her smile sits different—the same melody repeats, the guitar just slightly out of tune.

She didn't seem like herself, a shell of who she once was; her laugh no longer brought the same warmth as before. Her makeup felt different in a way he couldn't express, it was meant to change, why did it feel so strange? It wasn't just her, everybody he'd ever known felt, well, different. Each person blends together, stretched into the same lines, all lead to a path just out of his sight.

He couldn't cry, her plan would not allow for anything unintended. Cracks in the environment slowly revealed themselves, but as suddenly as they appeared, they were gone. How do you tell God that her love was never enough? No words are needed, for your meaning can not be expressed—the air fell thin, some things are better left understood.

A puppet can not feel the strings, so how could it ever mourn its freedom?
